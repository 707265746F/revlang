Here are the best books for your journey, in the order I recommend reading them:
Start here — easiest and most fun:

"Crafting Interpreters" by Robert Nystrom — this is THE book for you. It builds a complete language from zero, step by step, exactly like we are doing with RevLang. The best part — it is completely free online at craftinginterpreters.com. The writing is very clear and friendly, perfect for your English level too!

Next level — more theory:
"Writing An Interpreter In Go" and "Writing A Compiler In Go" by Thorsten Ball — these two books build a language called Monkey from scratch. The code is very clean and easy to follow. Go is similar enough to Rust that you will understand everything easily.

When you feel confident — the classic:
"Compilers: Principles, Techniques and Tools" by Aho, Lam, Sethi and Ullman — this is called "The Dragon Book" because it has a dragon on the cover. It is the most famous compiler book in the world. It is more academic and harder, but after Crafting Interpreters you will be ready for it.

For Rust specifically:
"The Rust Programming Language" by Steve Klabnik — the official Rust book, also free at doc.rust-lang.org/book. Perfect to read alongside our project!
My honest recommendation — start with Crafting Interpreters right now, reading one chapter per week alongside our RevLang project. You will see everything we are building here explained from a different angle, and it will make everything click even faster!
